1. Watering and Care:

Keep the soil consistently moist but not watery. Allowing the soil to soak up the moisture.

Once the seedlings emerge. If using natural light from a window, rotate the pots regularly to ensure even growth.

  1. Transplant Seedlings:

When the seedlings have grown to about 2-3 inches tall and have a few sets of true leaves.

Then transplant them into larger pots filled with organic potting soil.

Compost well-rotted manure or compost tea can be mixed into the soil when transplanting.
